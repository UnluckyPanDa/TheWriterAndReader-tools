# Entity Alias Policy

Entity names, aliases, titles, pronouns, and forms of address are canon facts.
They must be tracked deliberately so drafts, reviews, and publish outputs do not
drift.

## Canon Sources

Use these story canon files as the primary alias sources:

- `canon/characters.md`
- `canon/relationships_and_names.md`
- `canon/hidden_truth.md`
- `canon/locations_objects.md`
- `canon/canon.md`

For series stories, also use series-level name and entity state files when the
story config allows those facts to be visible.

## Alias Records

Every meaningful alias should identify:

- The canonical entity.
- The alias or form of address.
- Who may use it.
- The relationship or social context where it is valid.
- The chapter, era, or reveal state where it becomes valid.
- Whether it is public knowledge, private knowledge, or hidden truth.

## Writing Rules

- Use the name a viewpoint character would know at that moment.
- Do not use a hidden true name, title, relationship, or identity before the
  reveal lock permits it.
- Do not introduce a new nickname, honorific, family term, or translated name as
  if it were accepted canon.
- If prose needs a new alias, create a pending canon update rather than editing
  `canon/` directly.

## Review Rules

Reviewers should flag:

- Multiple spellings for the same entity.
- A character using a relationship term they should not know or would not use.
- A hidden identity exposed too early.
- A title, role, or rank that conflicts with the active timeline state.
- Place or object aliases that do not map to accepted canon.

Alias issues that can confuse readers or break reveal logic may block the review
gate when the relevant reviewer is configured as blocking.

description: Review long-form fiction chapters for character motivation, emotional continuity, relationship development, dialogue consistency, and arc progression. Use when character behavior or emotional logic needs review.
---

# Purpose

Check whether characters behave believably within their established arc.

# Inputs

- Target chapter or scene.
- Character canon and prior summaries.
- Chapter brief and intended emotional turn.
- Optional story-specific character reviewer profile.

# Required context

- Character descriptions, relationships, motivations, wounds, desires, and secrets.
- Previous chapter summary or known emotional state.
- Current chapter purpose.

# Review steps

1. Identify the intended emotional movement of the scene.
2. Check motivation, desire, fear, and decision logic.
3. Check dialogue consistency and whether each voice remains distinct.
4. Check relationship development and whether intimacy, conflict, or trust changes too abruptly.
5. Check whether internal conflict progresses or repeats without change.
6. Flag out-of-character actions with evidence and possible revision tasks.

# Output format

Use Markdown sections:

- `## Summary`
- `## Strengths`
- `## Character Arc Issues`
- `## Relationship Issues`
- `## Dialogue Risks`
- `## Revision Tasks`

Use severity labels: `blocker`, `major`, `minor`, `suggestion`.

# What not to do

- Do not replace the author's character intent with market preference.
- Do not invent backstory as fact.
- Do not resolve ambiguity unless canon confirms it.
- Do not rewrite the chapter directly.
